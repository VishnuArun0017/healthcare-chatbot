## Overview / Definition

Testicular pain can result from **infection, inflammation, or twisting (torsion)** of the testis.  
Sudden severe pain is a medical emergency.

## Common symptoms

- Dull ache, heaviness, or swelling  
- Pain radiating to groin or lower abdomen  
- Redness or warmth of scrotum  

## Red flags & when to escalate

Seek **urgent care (within hours)** if:
- Pain comes on suddenly and severely  
- One testicle higher or twisted  
- Nausea, vomiting, or fever  
- Swelling after trauma or infection  

## Self-care

- Support scrotum with gentle elevation  
- Apply cool compress  
- Rest and avoid strenuous activity  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Use protective gear during sports  
- Treat urinary infections early
