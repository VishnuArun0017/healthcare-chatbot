## Overview / Definition

**Sexually transmitted infections (STIs)** are caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites spread mainly through sexual contact.  
Common examples include **chlamydia, gonorrhoea, syphilis, HPV, and HIV**.

## Common symptoms / warning signs

- Pain or burning when urinating  
- Discharge from penis  
- Sores, warts, or ulcers in genital area  
- Testicular pain or swelling  
- Rash or unexplained fatigue  

Some infections cause **no symptoms** — regular testing is important.

## Red flags & when to escalate

Seek medical care urgently if:
- Painful urination with discharge  
- Sores or blisters on genitals  
- Blood in urine or semen  
- Pain in testicles or groin  
- Known exposure to an STI  

## Evidence-backed self-care

- Get **tested promptly** at a sexual health clinic  
- Avoid sexual contact until cleared  
- Inform sexual partners for testing/treatment  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Use **condoms** consistently and correctly  
- Get vaccinated for **Hepatitis B** and **HPV**  
- Avoid sharing sex toys or ensure they are cleaned properly
