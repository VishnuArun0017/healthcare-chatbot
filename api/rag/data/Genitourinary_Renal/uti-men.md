## Overview / Definition

UTIs in men are **less common** but often indicate an underlying cause such as obstruction or prostate issues.  

## Common symptoms / warning signs

- Burning when passing urine  
- Frequent or urgent need to pee  
- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine  
- Lower abdominal or pelvic discomfort  

## Red flags & when to escalate

Seek medical help if:
- Fever, chills, or pain in the back or side  
- Blood in urine  
- Difficulty starting or passing urine  
- Symptoms persist beyond 48 hours  

## Evidence-backed self-care

- **Hydration:** drink water regularly  
- **Avoid delaying urination**  
- Maintain genital hygiene  
- Avoid alcohol until symptoms improve  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Pee soon after sex  
- Keep hydrated  
- Manage underlying prostate or urinary issues  

## Special considerations

- Recurrent infections in men always need **medical evaluation**.  
- Older adults are more prone due to prostate enlargement.
