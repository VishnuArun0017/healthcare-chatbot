## Overview / Definition

A **migraine** is a headache disorder with **moderate to severe throbbing pain**, often on one side of the head.  
It may cause nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light or sound.

## Common symptoms / warning signs

- Headache lasting 4–72 hours  
- Nausea, vomiting  
- Sensitivity to light or sound  
- Visual aura (flashing lights, blind spots) before pain starts  

## Red flags & when to escalate

Seek **urgent care** if:
- First or worst headache of your life  
- Sudden severe headache (“thunderclap”)  
- Weakness, confusion, speech problems  
- Headache after injury or with fever  

## Self-care

- Rest in a quiet, dark room  
- Stay hydrated and eat regularly  
- Identify and avoid triggers (stress, missed meals, lack of sleep)  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Regular sleep and meals  
- Keep a migraine diary to identify triggers  
- Reduce caffeine and manage stress
