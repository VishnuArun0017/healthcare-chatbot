## Overview / Definition

A **seizure** is a sudden burst of electrical activity in the brain causing temporary loss of awareness, shaking, or unusual movements.

## Common symptoms

- Jerking or stiffening of limbs  
- Loss of consciousness or blank staring  
- Confusion after event  

## First Aid — What to Do

1. Stay with the person and keep them safe.  
2. Clear nearby objects to prevent injury.  
3. Place something soft under their head.  
4. Turn them onto their side once movements stop.  
5. Time the seizure — if **>5 minutes**, call emergency help.  
6. Do **not** restrain or put anything in their mouth.  

## Red flags

- Seizure lasting longer than 5 minutes  
- Repeated seizures without recovery  
- Breathing difficulty or injury during seizure  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Take prescribed medications regularly  
- Adequate sleep, stress control, and no missed doses
