## Overview / Definition

**Period pain (dysmenorrhea)** is cramping pain in the lower abdomen during menstruation.  
It is caused by **uterine contractions** as the lining sheds.

## Common symptoms

- Cramping in lower tummy or back  
- Headache, nausea, fatigue  
- Pain before or during the first 1–3 days of period  

## Red flags & when to escalate

- Pain suddenly worsens or lasts beyond usual days  
- Periods become heavier or irregular  
- Fever, discharge, or pelvic pain between periods  

## Self-care

- Apply **heat** (water bottle or patch)  
- **Gentle exercise** or stretching  
- Relaxation, rest, hydration  

## Prevention / lifestyle

- Regular physical activity  
- Balanced diet, avoid smoking and stress
